# Eduardo Mauer

**Founder and cultural systems architect of Associação MILK — Movimento de Intervenções e Linguagens Kulturais e Arte**  
Lisboa, Portugal

I build cultural infrastructures where territory, memory, language, education, art and public-interest technology meet. My current work is centred on the **Atlas Vivo MILK**, a living cultural atlas for Portugal designed as a public interface for memory, participation, accessibility and territorial imagination, supported by a protected methodological layer for research, governance and institutional interoperability.

## Core work

- **Atlas Vivo MILK** — public cultural cartography, territorial memory, folklore, festivities, chronicles, visual characters and participatory devices.
- **Folclore Vivo MILK** — methodological and curatorial framework for contemporary reinterpretation of Portuguese intangible heritage.
- **OCSR — Observatório de Coesão Social e Redes** — research layer for social cohesion, cultural complexity, public-interest evidence and interpretative governance.
- **Dialogic education and ludic methodology** — research line developed from my master’s dissertation on ludic language, dialogue, humour and teacher/student interaction.
- **Cultural systems and public interfaces** — web architecture, semantic governance, accessibility, open documentation, licensing and rights separation.

## Public repositories and records

- `eduardomauer/metodologias-culturais` — public technical documentation for Atlas Vivo MILK: APIs, interoperability, licences, OpenAPI, RGPD, accessibility and citation metadata.
- `milkivc/folclore-vivo-metodologia` — MILK repository reserved for the Folclore Vivo methodology and public dataset layer, pending write-access integration.
- Zenodo DOI already active for the academic/authorship layer: `10.5281/zenodo.20618922`.
- Target public domain for Atlas Vivo MILK: `atlas.associacaomilk.pt`.

## Principles

The public layer must be legible, accessible, reusable and citable. The protected layer must preserve authorship, consent, cultural responsibility, RGPD compliance, curatorial integrity and MILK’s intellectual property. Public documentation is opened only when it does not expose personal data, internal inference engines, sensitive methodology, private assets or unvalidated rights.
